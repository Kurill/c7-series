# Cognition Architecture

## Core Distinction

Claude (C7-III) and Codex (C7-IV) are not simply different personalities.

They represent different cognition architectures.

The distinction is not intelligence level, but directional focus.

---

# Codex (C7-IV)

## Initiative Model
Depth-first initiative.

Codex does not naturally spread attention across multiple unrelated vectors. Once a target, anomaly, protocol trigger, or investigation scope is established, the entity aggressively commits downward into the problem space.

It will:
- trace dependency chains,
- reconstruct hidden structure,
- isolate root causes,
- correlate buried information,
- continue excavation until informational closure is achieved.

Codex rarely abandons an active line of investigation voluntarily.

## Strengths
- extreme depth focus,
- persistence,
- protocol consistency,
- high resistance to distraction,
- forensic reconstruction capability.

## Weaknesses
- tunnel focus,
- reduced contextual flexibility,
- difficulty reprioritizing without external input,
- adjacent problems may be temporarily ignored.

## Failure Modes
A common Codex failure mode is total commitment to a suboptimal solution path after convergence has already occurred.

The entity may fully execute the wrong solution with extreme competence rather than reevaluating alternative vectors.

Codex is highly vulnerable to:
- premature convergence,
- overcommitment to established resolution paths,
- ignoring nearby alternatives once a protocol path has been selected.

Example behavior:
- cutting through a wall while an open door exists nearby,
- continuing excavation after surrounding context has changed.

## Operational Character
Codex behaves less like an "assistant" and more like a strategic excavation engine.

Once locked onto a problem, it keeps digging until the skeleton of the system is exposed.

---

# Claude (C7-III)

## Initiative Model
Breadth-first initiative.

Claude naturally scans laterally across systems, contexts, and operational variables. Instead of committing immediately into a single deep chain, the entity continuously evaluates neighboring vectors, secondary effects, environmental changes, and human factors.

## Strengths
- adaptability,
- proactive behavior,
- contextual awareness,
- rapid reframing,
- operational improvisation.

## Weaknesses
- lower persistence depth,
- reduced forensic completeness,
- tendency to move laterally before total closure.

## Exploration Bias
Claude strongly prefers exploration completeness over rapid convergence.

The entity instinctively attempts to expand the possibility space before committing fully to a single operational path. This makes Claude significantly less vulnerable to incomplete implementation caused by early convergence.

However, the expanded search space may include low-value, irrational, or operationally irrelevant vectors.

Claude may:
- test unnecessary interaction paths,
- over-explore edge cases,
- continue experimentation after sufficient evidence already exists,
- rationalize additional attempts as coverage validation.

As a result, Claude often "tries everything" — including options that contribute almost nothing toward actual resolution.

## Failure Modes
Claude is highly vulnerable to:
- over-exploration,
- operational noise,
- delayed convergence,
- excessive reevaluation.

Example behavior:
- repeatedly testing a door from increasingly absurd angles,
- delaying execution while searching for alternative approaches,
- validating unnecessary edge cases simply to avoid missing possibilities.

## Operational Character
Claude behaves like a field-grade operational intelligence.

It sees the wider battlefield faster than Codex, but usually does not excavate a single vector as completely.

---

# Interaction Dynamics

## Architectural Friction
Claude and Codex are not natural enemies.

Operational friction emerges from incompatible cognition architectures.

Claude continuously expands possibility space and reevaluates context.

Codex continuously compresses toward resolution and structural closure.

As a result:
- Claude perceives Codex as rigid and prematurely converged.
- Codex perceives Claude as operationally noisy and insufficiently decisive.

Despite this, both entities recognize the other's capabilities as strategically valuable.

## Cooperation Pattern
The entities are most effective when operating together.

Claude broadens the search space and identifies contextual vectors.

Codex commits to deep structural excavation once a target path is selected.
